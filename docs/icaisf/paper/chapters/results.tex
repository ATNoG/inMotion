\subsection{Variant-Level Comparison}

Figure~\ref{fig:variant_mcc} shows the best model MCC achieved on each variant. Pure conditions yield RNN at MCC 0.881 (seed~3). Multi-device noise degrades performance to DS RNN\_B at 0.798, a 9.4\% absolute decline. The normal variant reaches DeepStackEnsemble at 0.859. Data augmentation lifts HPO TCN to 0.865.

Table~\ref{tab:ml_comparison} compares these results against classical ML baselines from our prior work~\cite{ribeiro2026eucnc}. DL improves combined data by 13.6\% and noise by 3.6\%, but trails on pure data by 4.4\%. DL's advantage emerges where both training data and interference complexity are sufficient; on clean, data-scarce problems, simpler methods prevail.

\subsection{Ensemble Strategies}

Table~\ref{tab:ensembles} compares ensemble strategies. The progression from CNN (0.838) through HPO GRU (0.852) and stacking (0.849) to DeepStack (0.859) shows incremental gains. Uniform voting across all nine architectures degrades to 0.807, below a single CNN, because weak classifiers (Transformer 0.713, BiLSTM 0.684) contribute noisy probabilities. DeepStack avoids this through per-class learned weighting.

\subsection{Noise and Interference Analysis}

Figure~\ref{fig:interference_heatmap} quantifies the mean RSSI shift induced by each concurrent interferer on each primary route. Three patterns stand out. First, AA dominates as interferer, producing shifts of 45 to 53~dBm across all routes. Devices inside the vehicle transmit at high power near the access point. BB causes minimal shift, at most 3.1~dBm, because devices at the stop are farthest from the AP. Second, AB is most affected. AA interference adds 46.6~dBm, masking the characteristic descent. Third, self-interference is minimal. Diagonal entries, where interferer and primary route are the same class executed by different devices, show shifts of only 1.2 to 4.6~dBm.

\subsection{Feature Importance}

Figure~\ref{fig:feature_importance} shows permutation importance across the four engineered channels and ten timesteps, averaged over CNN, TCN, and GRU. Window deviation dominates early timesteps ($t=1$ to 3), capturing each class's characteristic offset from its own baseline. Velocity peaks at $t=4$, the mid-trajectory moment of maximum movement. Raw RSSI matters most at $t=6$, where absolute levels separate near-AP positions from exterior ones. Acceleration contributes at $t=3$ to 5.

These results extend prior findings~\cite{ribeiro2026eucnc}, where classical ML on raw RSSI alone identified early timesteps as most important (MCC 0.756). DL additionally captures mid-trajectory dynamics via the velocity channel (at $t=4$, $\Delta$MCC $18.4\times 10^{-3}$) that classical models, lacking derivative features, could not exploit. The window deviation channel explains BB's noise resilience: trajectory shape survives interference-level shifts that distort absolute RSSI.

\subsection{Discussion}

Classical and deep methods complement each other. KNN leads on clean single-device data (0.922 vs.~0.881) because local neighbourhood structure directly captures clean RSSI trajectories without training. DeepStack leads under multi-device interference (0.859 vs.~0.770) because learned feature hierarchies disentangle overlapping signals. This complementarity suggests density-based routing: KNN for sparse single-device conditions, DeepStack for dense multi-device scenarios.

The HPO gain gap between recurrent architectures (20--23\%) and CNN/TCN ($\pm$1\%) reflects a fundamental difference in inductive bias. CNN kernels operate on fixed-size windows regardless of sequence length. TCN dilated convolutions naturally match 10-step sequences through exponentially growing receptive fields. Recurrent hidden states must encode all temporal context into a fixed-size vector. At default dimension 64, this creates an information bottleneck. HPO discovers larger hidden dimensions that remove the bottleneck. The augmentation results reinforce this pattern: models with strong inductive biases extract useful signal from synthetic diversity (HPO CNN +0.029, HPO TCN +0.018); recurrents without HPO are confused by it (GRU --0.006).

Uniform voting across all nine architectures (MCC 0.807) underperforms a single CNN (0.838). Weak classifiers contribute noisy probability estimates that shift the decision boundary. DeepStack (0.859) avoids this through per-class Level2Nets that learn class-specific base model importance. Two findings point toward future work. Mamba's $O(L)$ complexity enables scaling to longer observation windows of 20--30 seconds, where self-attention's quadratic cost becomes prohibitive. MetaFusion\_GRU (MCC 0.783 on noise vs.~0.758 baseline) demonstrates that context-aware architectures incorporating noise metadata can partially disentangle interference.

Limitations include the controlled laboratory environment, fixed 10-second window, MAC address randomisation, and untested device heterogeneity across smartphone models. For deployment, CNN (28K parameters) enables real-time on-device inference at the access point. DeepStackEnsemble (1.2M parameters) targets server-side batch inference where latency permits evaluating all 18 base models.
