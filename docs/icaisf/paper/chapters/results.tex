\section{Experimental Results}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

Four dataset variants: Pure (128/32 train/test, isolated), Noise (960/240, four concurrent devices), Normal (1,802/451, combined), Augmented ($\sim$9,875/$\sim$240, synthetic training). 5-fold stratified CV, 80/20 splits. Seed~42 unless noted.

\subsection{Variant-Level Comparison}

Figure~\ref{fig:variant_mcc} shows best MCC per variant. Pure yields RNN 0.881 (seed~3). Noise degrades to DS~RNN\_B 0.798 (9.4\% drop). Normal reaches DeepStackEnsemble 0.859; augmentation lifts HPO~TCN to 0.865. Compared to classical ML~\cite{ribeiro2026eucnc}---GP 0.756 combined, KNN 0.922 pure, CatBoost 0.770 noise---DL improves combined ($+$13.6\%) and noise ($+$3.6\%), but trails on pure ($-$4.4\%) where 128 samples are insufficient for deep models. The improvement pattern---$+$13.6\% combined, $+$3.6\% noise, $-$4.4\% pure---reveals that DL's advantage is conditional: it requires both sufficient training data and interference complexity to justify its capacity. On clean, data-scarce problems, simpler methods prevail.

\subsection{Augmentation Ablation}

Table~\ref{tab:augmentation} shows augmentation impact. CNNs and TCNs benefit from synthetic diversity ($+$0.011--0.029 MCC), while recurrents may degrade slightly ($-$0.001--0.006). This asymmetry has a mechanistic explanation: synthetic Gaussian noise injected into 10-step sequences creates spurious local fluctuations that recurrent architectures interpret as temporal dependencies, while CNNs---with local receptive fields and weight sharing---naturally smooth these artifacts into the learned feature hierarchy. HPO-optimised models gain most, confirming that augmentation and HPO are synergistic for architectures with strong inductive biases.

\subsection{Ensemble Strategies}

Table~\ref{tab:ensembles} compares ensemble strategies. The progression from CNN (0.838) through HPO GRU (0.852) and stacking (0.849) to DeepStack (0.859) shows consistent gains. Uniform voting across all 9 architectures degrades to 0.807---below a single CNN---because weak classifiers (Transformer 0.713, BiLSTM 0.684) dilute the vote. DeepStack avoids this through learned per-class weighting.

\subsection{Feature Importance}

Figure~\ref{fig:feature_importance} shows permutation importance across 4 channels and 10 timesteps, averaged over CNN, TCN, and GRU. Window deviation dominates early timesteps ($t\!=\!1$--3), capturing class-specific offsets from the baseline. Velocity peaks at $t\!=\!4$, the mid-trajectory maximum. Raw RSSI matters at $t\!=\!6$; acceleration at $t\!=\!3$--5. This aligns with and extends prior findings~\cite{ribeiro2026eucnc}, where classical ML operating on raw RSSI alone identified early timesteps as most important (MCC 0.756). DL additionally captures mid-trajectory motion dynamics (velocity at $t\!=\!4$, $\Delta$MCC $18.4\times 10^{-3}$) that classical models, lacking temporal derivative features, could not exploit---contributing directly to the 13.6\% relative improvement. The window deviation channel, which encodes trajectory shape independent of absolute RSSI level, explains why BB (flat pattern) remains the most noise-resilient class: shape-based features survive interference-level shifts that distort absolute RSSI values.

\subsection{Discussion}

The complementary profiles of classical and deep methods---KNN leads on clean data (0.922 vs.~0.881), DeepStack on interference (0.859 vs.~0.770)---suggest density-based routing: KNN for sparse single-device conditions where local structure suffices, DeepStack for dense multi-device scenarios where learned feature hierarchies disentangle overlapping signals.

The HPO gain gap between recurrents ($+$20--23\%) and CNN/TCN ($\pm$1\%) reflects a fundamental architectural difference. CNN kernels operate on fixed-size windows; TCN dilated convolutions naturally match 10-step sequences. Recurrent hidden states must encode all context into a fixed-size vector---at default dim~64, this bottlenecks information flow. HPO discovers larger dimensions.

The augmentation results reinforce this narrative. Models with strong inductive biases extract signal from synthetic diversity (HPO CNN $+$0.029, HPO TCN $+$0.018), while recurrents without HPO are confused by it (GRU $-$0.006). The mechanism: synthetic noise creates spurious local fluctuations that recurrents interpret as temporal dependencies, while CNNs with weight sharing naturally smooth them. Augmentation and HPO are synergistic.

The ensemble comparison reveals why uniform voting fails: All-9 (MCC 0.807) underperforms a single CNN (0.838) because weak classifiers contribute noisy probabilities. Stacking (0.849) partially recovers, but DeepStack (0.859) achieves the best result through per-class Level2Nets that learn class-specific base model importance.

Mamba's $O(L)$ complexity makes it the natural choice for scaling to longer observation windows (20--30~s), where self-attention's quadratic cost becomes prohibitive. MetaFusion\_GRU (MCC 0.783 on noise vs.~0.758 baseline) demonstrates that context-aware architectures incorporating noise metadata can partially disentangle interference, pointing toward future designs that jointly model signal and environmental context.

Limitations include the controlled environment, fixed 10-second window, MAC randomisation, and untested device heterogeneity. CNN (28K params) suits on-device AP inference; DeepStackEnsemble (1.2M) targets server-side batch inference.
