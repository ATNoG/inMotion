\section{Materials and Methods}

\subsection{Dataset and Feature Engineering}

The dataset simulates public transport in a controlled environment~\cite{ribeiro2026eucnc}: Zone~A (vehicle interior, AP near door) and Zone~B (bus stop, corridor). Four devices execute four movement patterns---AA (inside), BB (at stop), BA (boarding), AB (alighting)---producing 10 RSSI readings at 1~s intervals. Pure (single-device, 160 samples) and noise (four concurrent devices, 1,200 samples) scenarios were collected. The combined dataset contains 2,253 samples: AA (407), AB (387), BA (399), BB (417). Four engineered channels per time step are extracted: raw RSSI $r_t$, velocity $\Delta_t = r_t - r_{t-1}$, acceleration $\Delta^2_t$, and window-deviation $d_t = r_t - \mu_w$ ($\mu_w$ = per-sample mean). These four channels capture complementary physical phenomena: absolute RSSI position, movement speed, acceleration, and trajectory shape relative to the per-sample baseline. Input tensors are $(N, 10, 4)$.

The DeepStackEnsemble uses per-class Level2Nets by design: confusion pairs such as AA$\leftrightarrow$BA (both near-AP) require different base model weighting than AB$\leftrightarrow$BB (both exterior). The residual skip connection preserves calibrated per-class logits while the MetaMLP corrects systematic biases.

\subsection{Augmentation and Implementation}

Class-conditioned Gaussian noise (clean: 6 copies, $\sigma=3.0$~dBm; noisy: 4 copies, $\sigma=0.8$~dBm) plus intra-class Mixup (600 pairs/class, $\lambda \sim \text{Beta}(0.4,0.4)$) expand training from 2,253 to 10,115 samples.

PyTorch~2.6, Optuna~4.7. Training: AdamW, cosine annealing, early stopping (patience~25), label smoothing ($\varepsilon=0.1$), Mixup ($\alpha=0.2$), L1 ($\lambda=10^{-5}$), L2 ($10^{-4}$), dropout ($p=0.3$), gradient clip (1.0), batch~64. HPO: 100 Optuna TPE trials/model; SoftMoE uses NSGA-II. Primary metric: MCC~\cite{chicco2020mcc}. 5-fold CV, 80/20 splits, 3 seeds. $\sim$65 models evaluated.
